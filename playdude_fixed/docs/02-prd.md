# Product Requirements Document

## Objective

Launch an editorial MVP for PlayDude that helps readers reduce uncertainty before visiting nightlife areas and venues in Bangkok, Pattaya, Tokyo, and Osaka.

## MVP User Problem

Nightlife decisions are high-uncertainty. Readers often do not know:

- Which area fits their intent
- Which venues are worth visiting
- What the atmosphere is like
- What prices, dress codes, timing, and etiquette to expect
- Whether a venue is comfortable for first-time visitors

## MVP Product Goals

1. Publish credible city-level nightlife guides.
2. Publish practical venue profiles.
3. Establish a repeatable founder-editor content workflow.
4. Create a structure that can later support membership, ads, and paid guides.
5. Build enough search-friendly content to validate organic demand.

## MVP Content Requirements

Each initial city should eventually include:

- City nightlife overview
- Best areas by intent
- First-night itinerary
- Venue profiles
- Budget and pricing guide
- Etiquette and safety notes
- Common mistakes
- FAQ

Each venue profile should include:

- Short summary
- Best for
- Not ideal for
- Atmosphere
- Crowd
- Location context
- Pricing signals
- Timing
- Entry expectations
- Practical tips
- Last verified date

## MVP Functional Requirements

The first website build should support:

- Static pages or CMS-backed editorial pages
- City guide pages
- Venue profile pages
- Category/tag navigation
- Search engine metadata
- Fast mobile browsing
- Clear internal linking between cities, areas, and venues

## MVP Non-Functional Requirements

- Mobile-first performance
- Clean URL structure
- Search-friendly HTML
- Simple content maintenance
- Editorial credibility signals
- Accessibility basics
- Easy future migration to paid content

## Out of Scope

- Paid memberships
- User-generated reviews
- Venue owner dashboards
- Live chat
- Booking integrations
- Real-time crowd data
- Mobile app

## Key Metrics

Early validation metrics:

- Organic search impressions
- Organic clicks
- City guide page views
- Venue profile page views
- Average engagement time
- Internal link click-through
- Newsletter or update signup interest, if added

## Risks

- Content becomes too broad and travel-blog-like
- Tone drifts toward adult or sensational positioning
- Venue information goes stale
- Publishing pace exceeds founder-editor quality control
- Content lacks enough specificity to be useful

## MVP Acceptance Criteria

The MVP is ready when:

- Each initial city has a useful guide structure
- At least one city has a strong flagship guide
- Venue profile template is implemented consistently
- Pages load quickly on mobile
- Editorial tone matches Nightlife Intelligence positioning
- The site can publish new guides without engineering work
