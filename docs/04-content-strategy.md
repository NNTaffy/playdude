# Content Strategy

## Editorial Mission

PlayDude helps readers understand nightlife before they arrive. Every article should answer the practical question: what should someone know before they go?

## Content Style

Target mix:

- 70% Lonely Planet: polished, contextual, researched, composed
- 30% Tripadvisor: practical, expectation-setting, grounded in visitor concerns

Avoid writing like:

- A party hype site
- A generic travel blog
- An adult directory
- A thin affiliate listicle
- A venue press release

## Core Content Pillars

1. City Intelligence

Explain how a city works at night: areas, timing, transport, atmosphere, costs, etiquette, and common mistakes.

2. Area Intelligence

Help readers choose the right neighborhood for their intent: relaxed drinks, high-energy clubs, local bars, international scenes, premium venues, or first-night exploration.

3. Venue Intelligence

Set expectations for specific places: who it suits, what it feels like, when to go, what it may cost, and what might surprise first-time visitors.

4. Practical Confidence

Reduce anxiety with concrete guidance: dress, arrival time, cover charges, payment habits, local norms, safety, and transport.

## Article Quality Checklist

Before publishing, each article should:

- Explain who the content is for
- Make clear recommendations without overclaiming
- Include practical details readers can act on
- Mention tradeoffs
- Avoid sensational language
- Include last updated or last verified date
- Link to related city, area, and venue pages

## Founder-Editor Workflow

1. Choose one city focus for the week.
2. Draft one flagship guide or three smaller profiles.
3. Verify core facts from multiple sources where possible.
4. Edit for tone and usefulness.
5. Add internal links.
6. Add last updated or last verified date.
7. Publish.
8. Track performance after indexing.

## Content Freshness

Nightlife information can become stale quickly. Use visible freshness signals:

- Last updated for guides
- Last verified for venue profiles
- Outdated notice when confidence drops
- Editorial notes when a venue changes format

## Editorial Red Lines

Do not publish:

- Unverified accusations
- Unsafe advice
- Explicit adult-service framing
- Degrading descriptions of workers, locals, or visitors
- Fake firsthand claims
- Pricing presented as exact when it is only approximate
